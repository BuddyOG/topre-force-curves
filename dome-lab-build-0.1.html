The Dome Lab.
Topre & EC, measured — not marketed.

Every dome is sold as a single number. Your fingers feel the whole curve. The Dome Lab is primary-source engineering for electrostatic capacitive keyboards: force curves from a calibrated bench, switch geometry traced from CAD of the real parts, and a parts catalog with the tolerance findings nobody publishes. All of it open, all of it interactive.

How the lab works

Measured, not quoted

Every force figure comes off a calibrated bench at 0.005 mm steps, averaged across runs. If it wasn't measured here, it's labeled.

Geometry from CAD

The Explorer's cross-section is sectioned from solid models of the actual parts — clearances read from the assembly, not assumed.

Open by default

Raw CSVs, the viewer, and the tools live in a public repo. Push a new test set and every tool picks it up automatically.

Credit where due

The bench is a build of bluepylons' open-source Open-Switch-Curve-Meter. Sources, patents, and designs are cited, always.
