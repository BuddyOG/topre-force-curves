Method

Press and return strokes are recorded at 0.005 mm displacement steps on a calibrated bench (~0.15 mm/s — quasi-static), with the load cell verified against standardized test weights. Every configuration is tested in multiple runs and averaged; run-to-run deviation in collapse force is under one gram, which is why grams and gf·mm are displayed as whole numbers. Hover the chart for readings as recorded.

Collapse is the tactile peak of the press curve. Valley is the post-collapse minimum. Snap % = (Collapse − Valley) / Collapse × 100 — the dome-switch industry's tactile-ratio standard, where linear = 0% and higher is crisper. Travel is detected where the bottom-out wall begins (press slope exceeding 300 g/mm past the valley). Total Energy is the area under the press curve from 0 to Travel, in gf·mm — the total work your finger does across the keystroke.
